## 2. SEO Best Practices

### Title Tag Optimization
- **Length:** 50‚Äì60 characters
- **Format:** `Primary Keyword | Brand` or `Primary Keyword ‚Äî Supporting Phrase | Brand`
- **Recommendations for Vizzion:**
  - `Solar Visualization Widget for Lead Generation | Vizzion`
  - `Turn Website Visitors Into Solar Leads | Vizzion.io`
  - `Solar Panel Visualizer Widget ‚Äî Capture Leads Automatically | Vizzion`

### Meta Description
- **Length:** 150‚Äì160 characters
- **Include:** Primary keyword, value proposition, CTA
- **Recommendation:** "Embed Vizzion's solar visualization widget on your website. Homeowners see panels on their roof ‚Äî you capture their email. Turn every visitor into a lead."

### Header Structure (H1‚ÄìH3)
- **H1:** Single, keyword-rich, benefit-driven headline (only one per page)
  - Example: "Turn Every Website Visitor Into a Qualified Solar Lead"
- **H2s:** Section headers that address key pain points and features
  - "How It Works"
  - "Why Solar Companies Choose Vizzion"
  - "Built-In Lead Capture"
  - "See It In Action"
  - "Pricing" / "Get Started"
- **H3s:** Sub-features, FAQ items, testimonial labels

### Content Length
- **Minimum 1,500 words** on the landing page (through combined copy across sections)
- **Ideal: 2,000‚Äì3,000 words** when including FAQ section, feature descriptions, and use cases
- Long-form content correlates with higher rankings for B2B SaaS queries
- Google rewards comprehensive, helpful content ‚Äî especially for YMYL-adjacent topics

### Schema Markup
Implement the following structured data:

1. **SoftwareApplication schema** ‚Äî name, description, applicationCategory, operatingSystem, offers
2. **Organization schema** ‚Äî name, url, logo, contactPoint
3. **FAQPage schema** ‚Äî critical for capturing featured snippets on "how to" queries
4. **Review/AggregateRating schema** ‚Äî if you have testimonials/ratings
5. **BreadcrumbList schema** ‚Äî for site navigation signals
6. **Video schema** ‚Äî if embedding a demo video (highly recommended)
7. **HowTo schema** ‚Äî for the "How It Works" section

### Technical SEO Essentials
- **Page speed:** Sub-3 second load time (critical for landing pages)
- **Mobile-first:** 60%+ of solar searches happen on mobile
- **Core Web Vitals:** LCP < 2.5s, FID < 100ms, CLS < 0.1
- **Image optimization:** WebP format, lazy loading, descriptive alt text with keywords
- **Internal linking:** Link from blog content to the solar landing page
- **URL structure:** `/solar` or `/solar-lead-generation` (short, keyword-rich)

### Link Building Opportunities
- Guest posts on solar industry publications (Solar Power World, PV Magazine)
- SEIA member directory listing
- Solar software comparison/review sites
- Home improvement and contractor marketing blogs
- SaaS directories (G2, Capterra, Product Hunt)

## 5. Solar Industry Pain Points

### The Macro Challenge: Lead Generation is the #1 Pain Point

Based on industry research, forums, competitor messaging, and SEIA data:

**The residential solar market is under pressure:**
- The 25D tax credit is ending/changing, creating uncertainty
- High interest rates have dampened demand since 2023
- California NEM 3.0 changes disrupted the largest market
- Customer acquisition costs (CAC) have skyrocketed ‚Äî averaging **$3,000‚Äì$6,000 per residential install**
- Lead quality from third-party sources (bought leads) is declining

### Top Pain Points Solar Installers Express

#### 1. "We're spending a fortune on leads that don't convert"
**Language they use:**
- "Garbage leads"
- "Tire kickers"
- "Leads that don't pick up the phone"
- "Paying $50+ per lead and only closing 5-10%"
- "Lead vendors are killing our margins"
- "We need EXCLUSIVE leads, not shared ones"

**Vizzion angle:** Your own website generates exclusive, self-qualified leads for free.

#### 2. "Our website doesn't generate any business"
**Language they use:**
- "We get traffic but no one fills out the form"
- "Nobody wants to give their info for a 'free quote'"
- "Our website is basically a brochure"
- "We're paying for SEO/Google Ads but not getting leads"

**Vizzion angle:** Transform your website from a digital brochure into a lead generation machine.

#### 3. "Homeowners don't understand what solar looks like on THEIR house"
**Language they use:**
- "They can't visualize it"
- "We need to show them what their roof would look like"
- "The biggest objection is aesthetic ‚Äî they're afraid it'll look ugly"
- "If they could see it, they'd buy it"

**Vizzion angle:** Let homeowners see solar on their own roof before you ever pick up the phone.

#### 4. "We're losing deals to competitors who engage faster"
**Language they use:**
- "Speed to lead is everything"
- "If we don't call back within 5 minutes, they've already talked to someone else"
- "The first company to engage wins"
- "We need to capture interest the moment they're thinking about solar"

**Vizzion angle:** Capture interest 24/7, the moment a homeowner is curious ‚Äî even at 2 AM on a Sunday.

#### 5. "It's hard to stand out from other solar companies"
**Language they use:**
- "Every solar company's website looks the same"
- "We all say the same things ‚Äî save money, go green"
- "How do we differentiate?"
- "We need something that makes us look cutting-edge"

**Vizzion angle:** Be the only solar company in your market with an interactive visualization tool on your website.

#### 6. "We can't scale our sales team fast enough"
**Language they use:**
- "Good solar reps are hard to find"
- "Training takes months"
- "We need more appointments, not more reps"
- "Our reps spend too much time on unqualified prospects"

**Vizzion angle:** Pre-qualify prospects before your sales team ever picks up the phone. Every lead has already shown interest by visualizing solar on their home.

#### 7. "Our close rate is dropping"
**Language they use:**
- "Close rates used to be 30-40%, now we're at 15-20%"
- "Homeowners are shopping 5-6 companies"
- "The market is more competitive than ever"
- "We need to build trust faster"

**Vizzion angle:** Leads who've already seen solar on their roof are warmer, more engaged, and easier to close.

### Industry-Specific Terms to Use in Copy
- **Lead to PTO** (Permission To Operate) ‚Äî the full solar customer journey
- **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- **Speed to lead** ‚Äî how fast you respond to an inquiry
- **Sit rate** ‚Äî percentage of leads that result in an in-home appointment
- **Close rate** ‚Äî percentage of appointments that become sales
- **Self-gen leads** ‚Äî leads generated by the company itself (vs. bought)
- **Exclusive leads** ‚Äî not shared with competitors
- **Adders** ‚Äî additional system components or upgrades
- **NEM** (Net Energy Metering) ‚Äî relevant policy term
- **ITC** (Investment Tax Credit) ‚Äî federal solar tax credit
