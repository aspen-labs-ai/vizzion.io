# Vizzion.io ‚Äî Fragmented Industry Market Research
## Beyond Home Improvement: Where the Widget Works

*Research compiled Feb 7, 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

Vizzion's "upload photo ‚Üí see result ‚Üí enter email ‚Üí get lead" widget has potential far beyond its current home improvement focus. The ideal target industry has: thousands of small businesses with websites, visual transformations that vary by customer, deal sizes above $500, and no existing great visualization tool.

After researching 20 industries across trades, personal services, events, agriculture, pets, hospitality, real estate, and retail ‚Äî I've identified **Tier 1 targets** (highest confidence), **Tier 2** (strong potential with caveats), and **Tier 3** (interesting but harder).

## TIER 1 ‚Äî HIGHEST CONFIDENCE (Go after these)

---

### 1. üöó Vehicle Wraps & Custom Auto Paint
**Visualization:** Customer uploads photo of their car/truck/van ‚Üí sees it with a custom wrap design, color change, or commercial branding applied.

- **US Businesses:** ~5,000‚Äì8,000 dedicated wrap shops (subset of 33,761 auto body/paint shops, NAICS 811121). Growing fast ‚Äî vehicle wrap market was $5B+ globally in 2024.
- **Average Deal Size:** $2,500‚Äì$5,000 for a full wrap; $1,000‚Äì$2,000 for partials. Commercial fleet wraps: $3,000‚Äì$10,000 per vehicle.
- **Current Lead Gen:** Instagram portfolios, Google/Yelp listings, word of mouth. **Broken because:** Customers can't envision THEIR vehicle with a specific design. Shops waste hours doing mockup renders in Photoshop/Illustrator for prospects who may not convert.
- **Would they pay $50‚Äì200/mo?** **YES ‚Äî Strong.** These businesses already spend on mockup labor ($50‚Äì100/hr designer time). A tool that automates this AND captures leads is a no-brainer. $150/mo is one partial wrap away from ROI.
- **Technical Feasibility:** **HIGH.** Vehicle wraps are ideal ‚Äî cars are well-defined 3D objects, the wrap is a surface overlay. AI can detect vehicle contours and apply color/pattern/graphics realistically. Companies like 3M already have virtual wrap tools but they're B2B-to-manufacturer, not for the local shop's website.

**‚≠ê WHY THIS IS #1:** Fragmented market of tech-savvy small businesses (they're already on Instagram), high ticket, clear visual transformation, shops literally do manual mockups today that this replaces.

### 2. üèóÔ∏è Fencing Contractors
**Visualization:** Customer uploads photo of their yard/property ‚Üí sees different fence styles (vinyl, wood, chain-link, iron, composite) installed along their property line.

- **US Businesses:** ~20,000‚Äì30,000 fencing contractors. AFA has 1,400+ member companies across 19 chapters, but the industry is much larger. NAICS 238990 (specialty trade) includes fencing among 46,275 businesses.
- **Average Deal Size:** $3,000‚Äì$8,000 for residential; $10,000‚Äì$50,000+ for commercial.
- **Current Lead Gen:** HomeAdvisor/Angi leads ($30‚Äì80 per lead, often shared with 3+ competitors), Google Ads, yard signs. **Broken because:** Leads from aggregators are expensive and low-quality (shared). Customers struggle to visualize fence styles on their specific property.
- **Would they pay $50‚Äì200/mo?** **YES.** At $5K average deal and $50+ lead cost from aggregators, even 1 extra closed deal per quarter from their own website makes this pay for itself many times over.
- **Technical Feasibility:** **HIGH.** Fence lines are linear and follow property boundaries. AI can detect the yard/ground plane and overlay fence segments along it. Simpler than most home reno visualizations.

### 3. üé® House Painting (Exterior + Interior)
**Visualization:** Customer uploads photo of their house exterior (or room interior) ‚Üí sees it repainted in selected colors.

- **US Businesses:** 30,209 verified painting & wall covering contractors (NAICS 238320).
- **Average Deal Size:** $3,000‚Äì$6,000 for exterior; $1,500‚Äì$4,000 for interior per room/area.
- **Current Lead Gen:** Google Ads, Angi/HomeAdvisor, Nextdoor, yard signs. **Broken because:** Color samples on tiny swatches ‚â† seeing your actual house in that color. The Sherwin-Williams/Benjamin Moore color visualizers exist but they're manufacturer tools, not lead gen tools. They don't capture emails for the painter.
- **Would they pay $50‚Äì200/mo?** **YES ‚Äî Strong.** 30K+ businesses, many already paying for digital marketing. A widget that helps a homeowner see colors on THEIR house and captures their email? Painters would love this. Differentiation from competitors who just show generic portfolios.
- **Technical Feasibility:** **VERY HIGH.** This is one of the most proven AI visualization use cases. Color transfer on surfaces is computationally well-understood. Multiple apps already do this (but as consumer-facing tools, not B2B lead gen widgets).

**Note:** Sherwin-Williams ColorSnap Visualizer and similar tools exist but they're brand-specific, not embeddable lead gen widgets for individual painting companies.
